## 1. Target profile filter (repeating from COMMERCIAL_PACK § C.1)

A target makes this list if and only if:

1. Fleet is between 8 and 40 aircraft (big enough to matter, small
   enough to move fast)
2. Primary base is in Europe (EASA regulatory posture matches our
   starting compliance stack)
3. Current onboard connectivity is either missing, paywalled, or
   publicly known to be underperforming
4. We can find at least one warm-introduction path through Wavult's
   Stockholm / Nordic network or through a shared investor / advisor
5. The chief pilot or head of flight operations is identifiable by
   name on LinkedIn or the company website
6. No active RFP with an incumbent connectivity vendor in the last
   12 months (if there is, we wait — don't walk into a procurement
   process we can't win)

Targets that do NOT make this list:

- Major flag carriers (KLM, Lufthansa main, SAS main, BA). They run
  18-month RFP cycles and they are where incumbents win.
- US operators. Different regulatory stack, different sales culture,
  wait for the EASA reference story.
- LCCs (Ryanair, Wizz, EasyJet, Norwegian). Connectivity is an
  ancillary revenue lever priced by the commercial team — wrong
  buyer, wrong economic model for our thesis.
- Cargo operators. No passengers.

## 4. Who we are looking for at each operator

We want one of these roles (in priority order):

1. **Director of Flight Operations** — owns the chief pilot, owns
   onboard passenger experience, has budget discretion on small items
2. **Chief Pilot** — line-operations authority, technical gatekeeper
3. **CFO / Commercial Director** — if the operator frames
   connectivity as a cost / revenue item rather than an ops item
4. **CIO / Head of IT** — only at larger operators; at Tier 1
   operators there usually isn't one and that's fine

We do NOT start with:

- Head of Marketing (wrong economic authority)
- Maintenance Manager (right side of the house but not the buyer)
- CEO (unless the operator has <10 employees, in which case the CEO
  IS the Director of Flight Ops)

## 6. Follow-up rules

- **No response after 5 business days** → one follow-up email, plain
  text, one line: "Bumping this once in case it got buried — still
  would love 30 minutes whenever works for you." Do not send more
  than one follow-up without a new reason.
- **Positive reply** → book the call within 48 hours. Do not let it
  slip to next week. Aviation buyers forget fast.
- **Polite no** → ask one question: "What would have to be true for
  this to be interesting a year from now?" Log the answer. Move on.
- **Hard no** → one-line thank-you, no argument, no escalation. You
  will see them again at EBACE.

## 10. What success looks like in the first 30 days of outreach

- 15 emails sent (all 15 targets)
- ≥8 first-meeting replies booked
- ≥5 first meetings held
- ≥2 technical deep-dives scheduled
- ≥1 paid-pilot LOI drafted

If the numerator on line 1 is less than 15 at day 14, the blocker is
not sales — it's that we do not yet have the 1-pager, the demo
video, or both. Do not send without them. One bad first impression
on a Stockholm bizav WhatsApp group kills the next 12 targets.
